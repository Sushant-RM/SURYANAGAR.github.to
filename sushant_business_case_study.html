Business Case Study: Digital Governance in Smart Cities

Overview Relevant SDGs Case Examples Conclusion

Overview

Digital governance in smart cities leverages technology to improve urban management, enhance citizen participation, and ensure sustainable development. This case study explores the role of digital governance in achieving specific UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Relevant SDGs

1. SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

  • Target 11.3: Enhance participatory urban planning and management.
  • Indicator 11.3.2: Proportion of cities with democratic participation in urban management.

2. SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions

  • Target 16.6: Develop transparent and accountable institutions.
  • Indicator 16.10.2: Guarantee public access to information.

3. SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure

  • Target 9.C: Increase access to ICT and digital solutions.
  • Indicator 9.C.1: Proportion of population covered by mobile networks.

Case Examples

1. Barcelona, Spain

Barcelona uses a smart city platform to enable citizen participation in urban planning, aligning with SDG 11.3.

2. Tallinn, Estonia

Tallinn's e-Governance system ensures transparent public service delivery, addressing SDG 16.6.

Conclusion

Digital governance in smart cities is pivotal for achieving sustainable development goals. By integrating technology with participatory governance, cities can create resilient and inclusive urban environments.
